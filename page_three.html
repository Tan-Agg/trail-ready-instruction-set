Step 5 - Fitting Your Pack

a

Hip Belt: Sit on the hip bones, or iliac crest. This is where 80% of the weight should be placed, not the shoulders. Tighten tightly.

b

Shoulder Straps: Shoulder straps should be anchored one inch below the shoulder crest. should not dig in, but rather rest on shoulders.

c

Load Lifters: Pull forward until the angle between the shoulder and the top of the frame is 45°.

d

Sternum Strap: two inches below the collarbone. Maintains straps in place; it doesn't support any weight.

Step 7 - Compression & Balance

To remove any empty space and stop the gear from shifting, tighten all of the side compression straps. Hold the pack by the haul loop so that it hangs straight. Repack till it is balanced from left to right if it tilts.

Nothing should move or wobble when you move around and gently bounce.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Heavy objects that are excessively low or far from the back
Packing more than 20% of your body weight
Loose compression straps
The headlamp is hidden in the main compartment.
Long term compression of a sleeping sack
Using the shoulder strap to lift the pack
